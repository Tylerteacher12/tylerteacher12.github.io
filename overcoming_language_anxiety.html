How do I deal with language anxiety whenever I speak English?

A popular question on Quora is How can I overcome foreign language anxiety when speaking English?.

Foreign language anxiety is a common problem for students learning English. It is a well researched topic in second language acquisition.While it tends to go away with experience, it is not uncommon for advanced learners of English to struggle with this feeling. Understanding self-efficacy and how to build it is key to reducing anxiety when speaking English.

What is self-efficacy?

Self-efficacy is a concept popularized by the late psychologist Albert Bandura. Basically, self-efficacy is the belief that you will be successful at a given task. For example, my self-efficacy for a task such as ordering a coffee in my native language is very high; I know I can do it! But my self-efficacy for ordering a coffee in Turkish (a language that I would love to study, but don’t know) is very low. I don’t think I would be successful.

This is a useful idea to refer to when thinking about foreign language anxiety. If I experience anxiety in a language that I am learning, it is usually because I don’t think I will be successful at a given task. I might feel anxious if I think I am going to make a mistake. I will certainly feel nervous when speaking to native speakers if I think they will judge me for my mistakes.

How to improve self-efficacy in a new language

Like most problems in language learning, improving your self-efficacy starts with practice. We feel anxious when we don’t think we can succeed. Therefore, a useful study habit is to make sure that you are practicing with materials that are challenging but not difficult. Learning something new is always a challenge, but the material should not be so difficult that you can’t succeed.

If you choose to work with a tutor, they will choose tasks you will be successful at. We can call these tasks success experiences. These experiences are extremely valuable for reducing anxiety because they give you positive memories to reflect on when feeling anxious. These experiences should be realistic enough that you will think “I know I can do this, I have done it before”.

You can also make your own success experiences outside of the classroom. Be conscious of when you are successfully communicating in the target language. This will give you positive memories to reflect on when you are feeling less confident in your abilities.

Find a role model

Our personal experiences are not the only thing that helps us build our self-efficacy. Humans are social creatures. We learn what is possible both from our experiences, and also from the experiences of others. This is true for learning foreign languages as well.

Chances are, you have met people who have mastered the language that you want to learn. Are these people similar to you? If you perceive them to be similar to yourself, it's a good idea to ask them about their experience as a learner. Chances are, they experienced the same challenges as you. If they learned English, so can you!

Finding role models are an excellent way to build self-efficacy. Learning a new language isn’t easy, but look–there are many people who have done it before. Surely, you can too. If you are feeling anxious in a particular situation, look for role models who have succeeded in the same arena.

Find support and opportunities to practice

The last factor that improves our language self-efficacy is positive feedback. Hearing positive comments about your abilities will help you to feel more confident in the language. At least, if you think these comments are genuine. Look for people to support you on your journey who will recognize your strengths and achievements.

Positive feedback can come from friends, strangers, or teachers. Take time to appreciate positive comments that you receive when using the new language. A tutor will look for opportunities to recognize your specific achievements, but so will many people that you meet. I’m always surprised how excited most people are when a foreigner tries to speak their language–so don’t be scared to make mistakes. A little effor goes a long way.
