"How can I learn English easily?"

Tyler Clock Feb 26 2022 0

“How can I learn English easily?”

I saw this question on Quora the other day. It is a question that I hear a lot on Cambly. The truth is that learning a language is not easy. At least, it’s not easy compared to most things we do in life. Learning a language takes time and disciplined practice. It’s much easier if you know how to practice effectively.

Steps to learning English

Here’s a few steps to get you started with your learning journey

Find a tutor who can assess your level.

Most likely, you already know some English if you are reading this article. A skilled tutor can help you to understand how well you speak, read, and write. Once you know your level, the tutor can help you design a learning plan for yourself. If you want to improve your speaking skills, a tutor can help you develop your skills through structured practice.

Pay attention to grammar

Grammar isn’t everything when learning a new language, but understanding it is essential. This doesn’t mean that you need to focus on finishing textbooks. There are many fun online resources for learning grammar. A tutor can help you to find these resources and choose what content is right for you.

Immersion is the most effective way to learn

The US Military is one of the most effective language teaching organizations on the planet. If you study with them for sixty-four weeks, there’s a 75-95% chance you will be fluent in a new language (depending on the language). How do they do it? By intense immersion.

Students in these programs take language classes for 6-7 hours a day. They do extra curricular activities in the new languages too, like cooking classes and field trips. Outside of class, they are advised to expose themselves to the new language as much as possible.

Very few of us who are learning on our own time, while working and balancing busy lives, can dedicate so much time to learning a new language. But that doesn’t mean that we can’t immerse ourselves. We can find time to listen and use English in our daily lives. Activities like listening to podcasts, watching movies, attending language exchanges, listening to music, playing video games, and reading poems and stories are all effective ways to practice independently. Plus, they’re fun!
